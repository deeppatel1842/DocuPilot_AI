## Chunking Strategies in RAG Pipelines

Chunking—the process of segmenting documents into manageable units for retrieval—is foundational to RAG performance. The choice of chunking strategy directly influences retrieval relevance, generation accuracy, and system efficiency.

### Types of Chunking Methods

1. **Fixed-Length Chunking**  
   This traditional approach segments documents into uniform, fixed-size windows (e.g., 512 tokens). While simple and computationally efficient, it often disregards semantic boundaries, potentially splitting coherent ideas and reducing contextual relevance [1][4].

2. **Semantic Chunking**  
   Semantic chunking aims to preserve coherent ideas within chunks by leveraging natural language understanding to identify meaningful boundaries such as paragraphs, topics, or discourse units. This approach improves contextual relevance and reduces hallucination in generated outputs [3][8].

3. **Structure-Aware Chunking**  
   Exploiting document hierarchy and formatting (e.g., headings, sections, tables), structure-aware chunking segments documents according to their inherent organization. Empirical studies demonstrate that this method enhances retrieval effectiveness and reduces computational overhead, especially in complex enterprise documents [5][7].

4. **Recursive and Overlapping Sliding Window Chunking**  
   Recursive chunking applies chunking hierarchically, breaking down large chunks into smaller sub-chunks. Overlapping sliding windows introduce controlled overlap between chunks to capture context continuity. These methods balance recall and precision but require careful tuning to optimize latency [4][8].

### Adaptive and Query-Aware Chunking

Recent advances emphasize **adaptive chunking**, where chunk granularity and method selection dynamically adjust based on document characteristics and query specificity:

- **Adaptive Chunking Frameworks**  
  Patents from Intuit and Dell (2025) describe systems that classify documents by structure, hierarchy, and content type, then select chunking strategies accordingly. For precise queries, the system retrieves targeted, fine-grained chunks; for vague queries, broader chunks provide wider context [2][3].

- **Query-Adaptive Semantic Chunking**  
  Incorporating user queries into chunk boundary determination, this approach scores chunk embeddings relative to the query and expands context windows dynamically. Rastogi (2026) reports 18–27% improvements in F1 scores over fixed or naive semantic chunking, highlighting the benefit of integrating query context into chunking [12].

- **Intrinsic Metrics for Chunking Selection**  
  Júnior et al. (2026) propose intrinsic metrics such as *References Completeness* and *Cohesion* to evaluate chunk quality, enabling dynamic selection of chunking methods per document without modifying underlying models or prompts [9].

## Reranking Trade-offs in RAG Systems

Reranking is the process of reordering retrieved chunks to prioritize the most relevant information before generation. It critically affects the reliability, accuracy, and latency of RAG outputs.

### Reranking Approaches

- **Embedding Similarity-Based Reranking**  
  Initial retrieval often uses vector similarity; reranking refines this by incorporating additional semantic or contextual signals [1].

- **Cross-Encoder Models**  
  More computationally intensive but accurate, cross-encoders jointly encode query and chunk pairs to produce fine-grained relevance scores, improving precision at the cost of latency [1][2].

- **Hybrid Reranking**  
  Combines fast approximate methods with selective cross-encoder reranking on top candidates to balance speed and accuracy [1].

### Trade-offs and Impact

- **Accuracy vs. Latency**  
  Higher reranking accuracy reduces hallucination and improves answer correctness but increases computational cost and response time, which is critical in enterprise settings requiring real-time or near-real-time responses.

- **Recall vs. Precision**  
  Aggressive reranking may improve precision but risk filtering out relevant but less obviously similar chunks, reducing recall and potentially missing critical context.

- **Hallucination Reduction**  
  Effective reranking prioritizes chunks with high factual relevance, mitigating hallucination in generative outputs, a key concern in enterprise AI applications [1][2].
