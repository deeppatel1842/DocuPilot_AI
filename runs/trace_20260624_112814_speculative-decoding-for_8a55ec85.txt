## 4. Key Findings and Results

### 4.1 Algorithms and Mechanisms

The core mechanism of speculative decoding involves two models:

- **Draft Model:** A smaller, faster model that proposes multiple candidate tokens per decoding step (typically 5–8 tokens).
- **Target Model:** The larger, more accurate LLM that verifies the draft tokens in parallel.

This parallel verification breaks the autoregressive bottleneck, allowing multiple tokens to be accepted or rejected simultaneously [4][9].

**Acceptance Rate and Throughput:** Typical acceptance rates hover around 60%, enabling speedups of 2× to 5× in wall-clock inference time. The draft model’s quality and latency critically influence overall performance.

**SubSpec Method:** This approach generates low-bit quantized substitute layers from offloaded portions of the target LLM, enabling lossless acceleration without retraining. It effectively reduces memory bandwidth bottlenecks during inference [6].

**Speculative Safety-Aware Decoding (SSD):** SSD incorporates safety constraints by using the draft model to prioritize safe or utility-optimized tokens, balancing speed and ethical considerations [7].

**Universal Speculative Decoding:** Recent advances allow pairing any small draft model with any large target model, even with heterogeneous vocabularies, broadening applicability [8].

### 4.2 Speedup Metrics

- **Typical Speedups:** 2× to 5× improvements in throughput have been reported across various LLMs, including GPT-family models and open-source alternatives [4][9].
- **Latency Reduction:** Wall-clock latency per token decreases proportionally with the number of tokens proposed per step and acceptance rate.
- **Hardware Impact:** Speedups are more pronounced on GPU and TPU architectures with high parallelism; CPU-based inference benefits less due to limited parallel compute [10].

### 4.3 Quality Trade-offs

- **Output Fidelity:** Speculative decoding maintains near-identical output quality compared to standard decoding, with negligible increases in perplexity or error rates when acceptance rates are high [4][9].
- **Error Propagation:** Rejected tokens require fallback to standard decoding, introducing minor latency spikes but preserving correctness.
- **Draft Model Selection:** A draft model too small or weak reduces acceptance rates, increasing verification overhead and potentially degrading quality.
- **Safety and Bias:** SSD methods demonstrate that safety-aware drafting can reduce harmful outputs without sacrificing speed [7].

### 4.4 Experimental Benchmarks

- Benchmarks on GPT-3 and Falcon models show consistent speedups with minimal quality loss.
